## What you can ask us about

### Preparing to buy a home

Organizing credit reports, identifying possible errors, reviewing collections, reducing high revolving balances, and preparing documents before lender review. See:

- [Mortgage credit repair](https://superiorcreditrepaironline.com/mortgage-credit-repair/)
- [Credit repair to buy a house](https://superiorcreditrepaironline.com/credit-repair-to-buy-a-house/)
- [Free homebuyer‑readiness credit analysis](https://superiorcreditrepaironline.com/free-credit-analysis-for-homebuyer-readiness/)

### Collections and medical collections

We help you review how a collection appears, identify the original creditor, and organize documents related to the account. See: [Collections before mortgage approval](https://superiorcreditrepaironline.com/collections-before-mortgage-approval/).

### Late payments

Compare reported late payments with statements, bank records, payment confirmations, hardship arrangements, and creditor correspondence. See: [Late payments before mortgage approval](https://superiorcreditrepaironline.com/late-payments-before-mortgage-approval/).

### High credit‑card utilization

High balances can affect your credit profile even when payments are on time. We review reported balances, limits, statement dates, and utilization patterns. See: [High utilization before mortgage approval](https://superiorcreditrepaironline.com/high-credit-utilization-before-mortgage-approval/).

### Charge‑offs, repossessions, and older accounts

These accounts may involve balance updates, collection activity, deficiency balances, duplicated reporting, or confusing status information. Reviews focus on each account's specific facts.

### Possible credit‑report errors

Errors may include incorrect balances, wrong payment status, unfamiliar or duplicate accounts, mixed‑file information, or identity details that do not belong to you. Disputes should be accurate, specific, and documented when possible.

## Before you contact us

Make the first conversation more productive by gathering:

- Current copies of your available credit reports
- Recent statements for accounts you want reviewed
- Collection letters or settlement agreements
- Payment confirmations and bank records
- Mortgage denial or adverse‑action notices
- Dispute submissions and investigation results
- Identity‑theft records, when applicable
- A short explanation of your goal and preferred timeline

Do not send original documents. Keep originals and provide copies only through secure, approved channels when requested.
